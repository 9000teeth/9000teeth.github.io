12 aug 2021

In the blue hours of today’s morning, I sat on the bed facing the wall. Sat with my arm propped up on my knee for balance. I saw the idle fingers just poised there, doing nothing, and the sloping curve of a forearm.

I thought to myself: That’s it, that’s all there is. I’m a boy. This is a boy’s arm I’m seeing, a boy’s idle fingers in the silence. I don’t know whose memory I was experiencing at the time.

03 aug 2021

How often have I wished to bow my head, praying, until my skull comes perfectly open and my brain drops out into my hands? From childhood I have felt the hole and the disparity.

I haven’t ever gotten the feeling that I inhabit my own body, that I belong to myself. Not one thought is original. Not one opinion is revolutionary. Even creativity’s a farce—I sift through wastepaper and I weep.

But if I could only hold my brain in my hands…

26 jul 2021

I feel so at peace in the comments section of the Youtube 24 hour vocal trance livestream. I do not speak Russian, but it can only be a sense of community I feel.

Everyone there seems so happy. Probably one or two of them, like me, live in some kind of brutalist accomodations with square windows spaced blissfully apart. The music reminds me of a room bleached and dimly glowing whiter than white, where urban twilight is urban twilight. И-ха.

10 jul 2021

“You shouldn’t let poets lie to you.”

I have been thinking about this quote recently. I first heard it said (you can look up the video clip if you’re interested) when I was about 14, and ever since I’ve been thinking that maybe this is where my distrust of poets came from.

So it’s just as simple as that: If anyone calls themself a poet, I have a hard time trusting them. I could say more, but there is no moral to this story.

19 jun 2021

June! Certainly, this is the only month that begins with new hope and ends not with longing but a kind of severance.

I have always felt most at peace in—most connected to—September: Wilt as presentiment, the blush of subtle gold not quite signifying what we both know will come. And November, too, its cold rain and sense of falsified nostalgia.

But what is June? I unclench my hand and find nothing there.
