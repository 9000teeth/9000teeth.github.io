1 jan 2022

Back home for a day and a night and part of a day. Not moving, just passing through. Most of my belongings are still in the car. Chicago is snowless, and here the blizzard has so thickened that you can really only feel either comforted or hopeless.

I began thinking up obituaries for myself:

(Claire/my childhood name) (x), aged (x), was found dead this morning of a self-inflicted gunshot wound. Traces of the prescription sedative Xanax were found in (his/her) bloodstream. Absent contravening evidence... well, no, I can’t say that. This isn’t a crime scene.

Mom was happy to see me. I was sleeping in my childhood bed in my childhood bedroom, which seemed smaller than ever. I thought it was my new heeled boots, but even after taking them off, I couldn’t fathom having spent every day of my life in that room.

I woke up but I didn’t move. Someone stopped in the doorway—I thought it was my mom. It was my dad, and he sighed very quietly and left. I can only tell you this with any certainty because he used to do this all the time, if you could believe me.

When I finally got out of bed, I thought it was morning, but I went to the kitchen and saw that it was ten past one in the afternoon.

31 dec 2021

B said something of which I remember only the gist: She had a friend she met when they were both 5, he did a certain stupid thing, he got mad and/or she told him off, she said of the whole event “No thanks, I didn’t need your friendship that much, I’ve known you since we were 5 but that’s over” or “You wanna fuck up that bad, I’m out, goodbye”. I don’t remember exactly. I was eating, watching the clock.

(A’s first words about B: B had taken the night shift because she didn’t get along with anyone on the day shift. A apologized for gossiping.

(B’s first words about A, to me: “that little girl needs to stop leaving her hair and dirty masks everywhere, shit’s disgusting”;

about S: “that guy really pisses me off sometimes, I was back at the register and he screamed at me to—”

(later, on Christmas, when I had an earlier shift: “I can’t believe you’re leaving me alone for 5 hours with that fucker”);

about R: “I gotta do this, I guess, or else I’ll hear it from his fat ass up there, even though I’ve been written up enough that I’ve long stopped giving a shit—fire me, I don’t care, you think I can’t find someplace that respects me?”;

about V, slowing her gait and rolling her eyes and lowering her voice as V questioned R about coupons in the background: “Oh my God, shut the fuck up”;

about H: “that guy’s a weird bird. He doesn’t really have a personality, so I don’t say much to him.”

(I saw her talking with H a few weeks back and they were both smiling—but then H inexplicably disappeared for a while. Gaping holes where his shifts were. When B brought this up with me, she referred to H as “that other guy”, forgetting, initially, his name. “Every time I ask about him,” she said, “they just go silent, they won’t tell me anything.”)))

We were eating and there were 8 minutes left on the clock. B had microwaved a bowl of instant soup that was already nearly empty when she uncovered its plastic lid. There was a red bag of Doritos which she’d brought, too, tucked inside a plastic grocery bag.

B sat to my left, a seat’s space between us. Maybe two. She told me there are a lot of cunts in the world, a lot of assholes. She told me to stand up for myself. She told me never to take anything lying down.

31 oct 2021

Took the train to get Thai food, but the train was over 15 minutes delayed. I had a nice one-two-goodbye exchange with a commuter waiting for the same train. A group of (students?) wearing Halloween costumes was on the opposite platform—somebody was dressed as Zelda.

I liked the restaurant, even though I forgot how sweet some Thai food can be. A nice surprise, I think! The bookstore next door was closed for the night.

29 sep 2021

I haven’t drunk much water in a while. I feel bloated and absurd almost all the time.

It reminds me, on some level, of the psychosis/drug-perpetuated haze I found myself in three years ago. Also autumn then.

But what to do about it? I can’t start running today. It’s night, I have work to do, my memory falters.

I need to buy baking soda tomorrow. Perhaps other vegetables.

03 aug 2021

How often have I wished to bow my head until my skull comes open and my brain drops out into my hands? From childhood I have felt the hole and the disparity.

I haven’t ever gotten the feeling that I inhabit my own body, that I belong to myself. Not one thought is original. Not one opinion is revolutionary. Even creativity’s a farce—I sift through wastepaper and I weep.

But if I could only hold my brain in my hands…
