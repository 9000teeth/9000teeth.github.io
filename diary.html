9 jun 2022

Moved. Elsewhere. Across my building is a Korean barbecue restaurant with another apartment of sorts on the second floor. As late as eleven o’clock—and, I guess, even later—I see someone sitting or walking there, curtains open, a yellowish light on.

Every weekday from late morning to early evening, a trumpeter plays in front of the community center. Same days, same time, at the Wentworth intersection, a man sings with a karaoke machine on which he has taped a note reading “thank you for your support” in two languages. Maybe it is just my imagination, but I get the impression that every day these two performers move closer and closer to each other. This might end violently.

A lot of people smoke behind the dumpsters here, a lot of men. Everyone is so beautiful.

1 jan 2022

Back home for a day and a night and part of a day. Not moving, just passing through. Most of my belongings are still in the car. Chicago is snowless, and here the blizzard has so thickened that you can really only feel either comforted or hopeless.

I began thinking up obituaries for myself:

(Claire/my childhood name) (x), aged (x), was found dead this morning of a self-inflicted gunshot wound. Traces of the prescription sedative Xanax were found in (his/her) bloodstream. Absent contravening evidence... well, no, I can’t say that. This isn’t a crime scene.

Mom was happy to see me. I was sleeping in my childhood bed in my childhood bedroom, which seemed smaller than ever. I thought it was my new heeled boots, but even after taking them off, I couldn’t fathom having spent every day of my life in that room.

I woke up but I didn’t move. Someone stopped in the doorway—I thought it was my mom. It was my dad, and he sighed very quietly and left. I can only tell you this with any certainty because he used to do this all the time, if you could believe me.

When I finally got out of bed, I thought it was morning, but I went to the kitchen and saw that it was ten past one in the afternoon.

31 dec 2021

Today, YouTube’s algorithm favors long videos that flower into long retention times, such that video essayists—running the gamut from ostensibly political to outwardly so—have gained favor among a generation which grew up with easy access to multimedia forms of expression and, now in their late teens and early twenties, takes a certain interest in these forms as education.

The lifestyle vloggers must be on Instagram now, or else they are dead. In southern California waving, smiling, shutting off their cameras. What they never show are the long hours afterward of sitting alone to rewatch and edit and critique, silently, these scenes, which are morbid in a way we all recognize too intimately to call morbid.

I once saw a video diary in which the editor made a mistake: They left in footage of the vlogger boring holes through their viewfinder, sighing at times, eager (if I could call it eager) to get on to the next exciting scene. The light from the computer monitor was harsh on their face, pale, and in that grainy half-moment I did not recall seeing the lens reflected back in their eyes.

03 aug 2021

How often have I wished to bow my head until my skull comes open and my brain drops out into my hands? From childhood I have felt the hole and the disparity.

I haven’t ever gotten the feeling that I inhabit my own body, that I belong to myself. Not one thought is original. Not one opinion is revolutionary. Even creativity’s a farce—the last pictures in my phone are of roadkill; the lake; the lake with its unique colors, the veneer of fog, digitally purged; the lake blurred into three colors; the lake and what it does to people and what it returns to them, washed up and dead, road-killed, lake-killed; and my tit.

But if I could only hold my brain in my hands…
